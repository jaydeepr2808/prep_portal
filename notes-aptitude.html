Quantitative Aptitude: Time & Work

Master one of the most frequent topics in aptitude tests.

Key Concepts

Work done is usually considered as 1 unit. If a person can do a piece of work in 'n' days, then that person's 1 day's work = 1/n.

Work Done = Time Taken × Rate of Work

Important Formulas

  • Efficiency: If A is twice as good a workman as B, then the ratio of work done by A and B is 2:1.
  • Shared Work: If A can do work in 'a' days and B in 'b' days, then together they can do it in 1 / (1/a + 1/b) = (ab) / (a+b) days.
Example: A can do a work in 15 days and B in 20 days. If they work on it together for 4 days, then the fraction of the work that is left is:

A's 1 day's work = 1/15;
B's 1 day's work = 1/20;
(A + B)'s 1 day's work = (1/15 + 1/20) = 7/60.
(A + B)'s 4 day's work = (7/60 * 4) = 7/15.
Therefore, work left = (1 - 7/15) = 8/15.
